Key Activities & Implementation

üå± Soil Preparation

The garden plot was prepared by clearing the designated area, testing the soil composition, and amending it with organic compost to ensure optimal growing conditions for the herbal plants.

üåø Planting

Ten varieties of common and beneficial herbal plants were selected and planted, including mint, basil, rosemary, lavender, chamomile, and other medicinal herbs using seedlings from local nurseries.

üíß Maintenance

Regular watering, weeding, and pest management were performed to ensure healthy plant growth. A maintenance schedule was created for the community garden supervisor.

üìä Monitoring

Plant growth was monitored weekly, with detailed records maintained in activity logs. Observations about plant health, pest issues, and seasonal changes were documented.

Workshop 1: Basics of Herbal Plant Care

Focus: Proper soil preparation, lighting requirements, and harvesting techniques for optimal plant health and yield.

Workshop 2: Herbal Identification and Uses

Focus: Distinguishing key herbs by appearance, understanding their traditional applications in culinary and medicinal contexts, and safety considerations.

Workshop 3: Simple Herbal Preparations

Focus: Hands-on demonstrations on making herbal teas, tinctures, infused oils, and other simple preparations for personal wellness.

üìñ Local Herbal Guide Pamphlet

A comprehensive "Local Herbal Guide" pamphlet was created, printed, and distributed to community members. The guide includes:

  • Identification guide for the ten herb varieties
  • Growing instructions and care tips
  • Traditional and modern uses
  • Safety precautions and contraindications
  • Simple recipes for herbal preparations

Project Outcomes & Impact

üéØ Knowledge Transfer

Over 50 community members gained practical knowledge about growing and utilizing medicinal and culinary herbs, with increased awareness about sustainable agricultural practices and natural wellness approaches.

üå± Garden Establishment

Successfully established a 4-square-meter demonstration herb garden with 10 varieties of plants, serving as a living resource and educational tool for the community.

üìñ Resource Creation

Developed and distributed comprehensive educational materials including the Local Herbal Guide pamphlet, recipe cards, and care instruction sheets to ensure long-term community benefit.

ü§ù Community Engagement

Fostered stronger community connections through collaborative gardening activities, knowledge-sharing workshops, and the creation of a sustainable resource for ongoing education.

Key Learning & Reflection

This project demonstrated that simplicity and hands-on engagement are key to effective community education. The community responded most positively to practical demonstrations and easy-to-understand resources. By focusing on achievable goals and sustainable practices, we created a lasting impact that extends beyond the project duration.
